DESCRIPTION
The Ailanthus altissima, known as the Tree of Heaven, is a species native to China and introduced in Europe in the 18th century. While appreciated for its extraordinary resilience, it is also feared for its highly invasive nature, as it spreads quickly, disrupts local ecosystems, and damages urban infrastructure. This ambiguous reputation highlights the tension between human attempts at control and the persistent challenge of coexisting with what defies management. "Tree of Heaven or Hell?" explores this debate by analyzing 76 YouTube videos and 925 comments, revealing how public discussions around Ailanthus reflect broader power dynamics between humans and nature. The project guides the user through three artifacts that illustrate the complex digital perception of the Ailanthus: a supercut of videos introducing the plant, a scrolling LED display that visualizes the tone and polarization of comments, and a catalog of methods suggested by users to eradicate the species.
