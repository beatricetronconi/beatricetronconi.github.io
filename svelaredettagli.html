DESCRIPTION
Svelare Dettagli is a project focused on accessibility in contemporary art, developed through the analysis of three artworks from Villa Panza collection in Varese. The concept is based on the idea that the true details of an artwork emerge only by getting closer and changing perspective: what appears uniform at first glance reveals complexity and richness when observed more carefully. Through an accessible video and a series of tactile artefacts, the project invites the audience to move beyond a superficial reading and discover what lies beneath the surface, making art accessible to people who are blind or visually impaired. Svelare Dettagli creates an experience where visitors become active participants, discovering with both eyes and hands the dual nature of each piece: what is visible from afar and what is revealed only through attention and curiosity. In this way, the project reflects on the importance of accessibility and the need to design inclusive experiences, allowing everyone to fully engage with contemporary art.
